I'm developing my Python, SQL, JavaScript, and CSS through practical work and personal
projects. I'm not an expert in any of them, and I'd rather be honest about that than
pretend otherwise.

Writing about it while I'm in the middle of it is a deliberate choice. Explaining
something — even badly — forces me to understand it more clearly than just reading does.
It also leaves a trail I can look back on to see how my thinking changed.

The risk is obvious: some of what I write now will look naïve later. I think that's fine.
A learning note is a snapshot of where I was, not a claim to authority. If anything, the
rough notes are the useful ones.

So expect some of the writing here to be tentative. That's the point. I'm learning in
public, and the notes are part of the work.
